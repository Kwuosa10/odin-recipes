Nigerian Pepper Soup recipe (goat meat version, but you can swap with chicken, catfish, or beef).

Description

Pepper soup is a spicy, flavorful West African dish made with meat (goat, chicken, or fish) or offals, cooked in a broth flavored with hot peppers, herbs, and spices like ginger, garlic, thyme, and locust beans. It's known for its rich, aromatic taste and is often served as a comforting dish or remedy for colds.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg goat meat (cut into chunks, or use chicken/catfish)
  • 2 tbsp pepper soup spice mix (you can buy or blend your own: uziza seeds, ehuru [calabash nutmeg], uda pod, alligator pepper, cloves)
  • 2–3 Scotch bonnet peppers (fresh or ground) – adjust to taste
  • 1 medium onion (sliced or blended)
  • 2 seasoning cubes (Maggi/Knorr)
  • 2 tsp ground crayfish (optional)
  • 1 tbsp ground dried pepper (optional, for extra heat)
  • 1 handful scent leaves (or substitute with basil/parsley if not available)
  • 1 tsp salt (to taste)
  • 6 cups water

Steps

  1. Rinse goat meat pieces.
  2. Place in a pot, season with salt, 1 seasoning cube, and half the sliced/blended onion.
  3. Add a little water, cover, and allow to steam on medium heat for about 10 minutes.
  4. Add about 5–6 cups of water (depending on how soupy you want it).
  5. Add remaining onion, crayfish (if using), and pepper soup spice mix.
  6. Stir and allow to cook until the meat is tender (about 40 minutes for goat meat; less for chicken or fish).
  7. Add fresh ground Scotch bonnet (ata rodo) and adjust seasoning with more salt/seasoning cube if needed.
  8. Taste and adjust for spiciness and flavor.
  9. Once the meat is soft and the broth is rich, add chopped scent leaves.
  10. Let simmer for another 5 minutes, then turn off the heat.
  11. Best enjoyed hot with white rice, yam, plantain, or just as a warming soup.

✨ Tip: If using catfish, don’t parboil it first—add the washed fish directly into the boiling broth after seasoning, so it doesn’t break apart.
